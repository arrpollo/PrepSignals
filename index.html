About PrepSignals

PrepSignals turns hundreds of real GMAT debriefs into a simple question: what did people who hit your target score actually do? Answer four quick questions on My Score Path, or pick a score range under Explore the Data to see the tactics that show up most, the resources people leaned on, and the individual stories behind every number.

Where the data comes from

Every debrief links back to its original public post on Reddit's r/GMAT and GMAT Club. We only summarise — the source is always one tap away so you can read it in full and judge for yourself.

Independence

PrepSignals is independent and isn't affiliated with GMAC, any prep company, or any course. Resources are named because the original posters named them, not because anyone paid to appear. Debriefs that read like promotions are flagged "Maybe Promo" so you can weigh them.

How to read the colors

Colors mean the same thing everywhere on the site: blue is Quant, purple is Verbal, teal is Data Insights, amber is resources, green is timing and test-day execution, and coral is score gains.

Accounts & privacy

You can use most of PrepSignals without an account: browsing debriefs, Explore, and building a score path all work anonymously, and anonymous activity stays in your own browser. A free account is needed to save things — debriefs, your checklist, and your progress log — so they can sync across devices. If you sign up, your name, email, and saved study data are stored securely with our database provider (Supabase); your password is stored only as a salted hash, and we verify your email before any data syncs.

We use Vercel's privacy-friendly aggregate analytics and PostHog product analytics to understand which pages and features are useful. We never sell your data, and marketing email is strictly opt-in. Full details: Privacy Policy · Terms of Service.
